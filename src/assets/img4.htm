27 Iconic Places to Visit in Mumbai

Mumbai is known for its nightlife and fast-paced living but it is also home to some wonderful landmarks. The financial capital of India is a heaven for those who love touristy sightseeing with iconic structures and historic locations dotting the city. From beautiful blue seas to architectural marvels of the bygone era, Mumbai has it all. Here are our picks of the top 27 places to visit in Mumbai.

Quick Facts About Mumbai:

  • Airport in Mumbai: Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj International Airport, Mumbai 
  • Top Railway Stations in Mumbai: Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus, Lokmanya Tilak Terminus, Mumbai Central, Dadar Junction, Kalyan Junction
  • Best Time to Visit: November to February and June to October
  • Travel Recommendation: You should spend at least 3 days to visit all the places to visit in Mumbai.

List of Best Places to Visit in Mumbai with Family

1. Gateway Of India – A Famous Landmark in Mumbai

The Gateway of India was erected to commemorate the arrival of King George V and Queen Mary in Mumbai. Check out the lovely Roman triumphal arch in the centre of the structure and the lovely designs on its walls.

From the Gateway, you can catch stunning views of the iconic Taj Mahal Palace hotel that is just opposite the road. The place is safe to visit at night and during the day, you can take a short boat ride from here.

  • Entry Fee: Free
  • Location: Apollo Bunder, Colaba  

You may also like:20 amazing street foods in Mumbai you must try at night!

2. Mumbai Port Trust Garden – One of the Most Beautiful Gardens in Mumbai

The Mumbai Port Trust Garden or Sagar Upvan is probably one of the most scenic gardens in Mumbai. Surrounded by greenery, the pathways at the garden are flanked on either side by old trees. The garden boasts a variety of medicinal and oriental plants as well as a few cacti. There is a rock garden here, too.

  • Entry fee: Rs. 5 per person
  • Location: Azad Nagar, Colaba
  • Timings: 6 am to 11 am

You mal also read about 15 fun things to do in Mumbai for youngsters

3. St. Thomas Cathedral – One of the Oldest Churches in Mumbai

The St. Thomas Cathedral on DN Road is one of the oldest churches in Mumbai. The church was built in true neo-gothic architectural style with tall pillars. In the church, you will find many epitaphs of soldiers who died fighting in the war, as well as beautiful stained glass windows.

At this Anglican cathedral, you will also find a memorial dedicated to the first bishop of Mumbai. One of the most famous places in Mumbai.

  • Entry Fee: Free
  • Location: Fort Veer Nariman Road, Fort
  • Timings: 7 am to 6 pm

Read more about 19 awesome night hangout places in Mumbai

4. Mani Bhavan Gandhi Sangrahalaya –A Museum Dedicated to Mahatma Gandhi

The Mani Bhavan Gandhi Sangrahalaya was previously the residence of Mahatma Gandhi’s good friend Revashankar Jagjeevan Jhaveri. The two-storey structure has now been converted into a museum that contains a few belongings of the father of the nation. Visit this place to learn about Gandhi’s life and his legacy.

  • Entry Fee: Free
  • Location: Laburnum Road, Gamdevi
  • Timings: 9:30 am to 5:30 pm

Also, check out the 12 best wholesale markets in Mumbai to shop like crazy!

5. Shree Siddhivinayak Temple – Famous Ganpati Temple in Mumbai

Located in the suburb of Prabhadevi, the Shree Siddhivinayak temple is dedicated to Lord Siddhivinayak. The best time to visit this iconic temple is on a Tuesday when special devotions are held. Devotees from all over Mumbai walk to the temple to offer their prayers to God. It is believed that praying at the temple can grant miracles if you pray with faith.

  • Entry Fee: Free
  • Location: Prabhadevi
  • Timings: 5:30 am to 10:00 pm

You may also read about 14 best temples in Mumbai to visit to experience a spiritual journey

6. Haji Ali Dargah – A Mosque in the Middle of the Sea

Haji Ali is a mosque and a dargah located off the coast of Worli. Situated in the middle of the sea, this mosque was built in memoriam of Pir Haji Ali Shah Bukhari. Qawwali sessions are held in the afternoons at the mosque and special prayers are held on Friday. The entire mosque is built in an Indo-Islamic architectural style.  

  • Entry Fee: None
  • Location: Haji Ali
  • Timings: 6 am to 10 pm

7. Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Vastu Sangrahalaya (Prince Of Wales Museum)

Situated inside a gorgeous building that was constructed using Indian, Mughal and British engineering styles, the Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Vastu Sangrahalaya (Prince Of Wales Museum) houses important artefacts and relics relating to the history of India. A visit to this museum is a great experience to learn about India’s rich and glorious past.

  • Entry Fee: Rs. 30
  • Location: Fort
  • Timings: 10:15 am to 6 pm

8. Mahalaxmi Temple – A Temple Dedicated to Goddess Mahalaxmi

This religious monument is an awesome structure. Dedicated to goddess Mahalaxmi, the temple has idols of Laxmi, Kali and Saraswati. It attracts many devotees who come to pray for wealth and prosperity. In the temple complex, there are small shops selling puja items and religious artefacts.

  • Entry Fee: None
  • Location: Mahalaxmi
  • Timings: 6 am to 10 pm

9. Global Vipassana Pagoda – A Meditation Centre in Gorai

A towering structure, the Global Vipassana Pagoda is a Buddhist meditation centre in Gorai. To get to the pagoda you have to take a ferry from the Gorai creek. Inside the temple, there is a meditation centre that is a haven of peace and silence. A visit here is a refreshing break from the noise and fast pace of Mumbai’s city life.

  • Entry Fee: Free
  • Location: Gorai

List of Best Places to Visit in Mumbai For Couples

1. St. George’s Fort – An Old Fort in South Mumbai

Built in the year 1769, the St. George’s Fort was once a glorious fort in Mumbai. Today, it lies in ruins, but it still exalts some of its former glory. It was built in honour of King George and was used as an arms station and to protect the empire from attack. The famous St. George’s Hospital lies next door.

  • Entry Fee: Free
  • Location: CST Area, Fort 

You can also read about the 29 top hidden places in Mumbai you should visit!

2. Kamla Nehru Park – An Iconic Garden in Mumbai

Located in South Mumbai, Kamla Nehru Park is a must-visit if you want to spend some quiet time with your partner. Surrounded by beautiful flowers and plants, the highlight at the garden is a giant shoe. The perfect time to visit the garden is in the evening at the time of sunset.

  • Entry Fee: Free
  • Location: Malabar Hill
  • Timings: 5 am – 9 pm

3. Jijamata Udyaan –Mumbai Zoo

Jijamata Udyaan is Mumbai’s only zoo where you can see animals such as lions, tigers, monkeys, alligators and deer. You can opt for a guided tour of the zoo, in case you are keen to learn more about the animals.

  • Entry Fee: Rs. 50 per head, Rs. 25 for kids
  • Location: Byculla
  • Timings: 9 am – 5 pm

You can also visit these 12 beautiful bird sanctuaries in Mumbai during your stay

4. Hanging Gardens –A Gorgeous Garden in Malabar Hill

A perfect place to relax, Hanging Gardens is surrounded by lush greenery and lovely flowers. It is situated opposite Kamla Nehru Garden and is a popular meeting place for couples. In the evenings, many families visit here to enjoy nature and take long walks on the lovely walkways.

  • Entry Fee: Free
  • Location: Malabar Hill
  • Timings: 5 am – 9 pm

5. Bandra Fort – Castella de Aguada

Popular with the young Mumbai crowd, a majority of this fort now lies in ruins. Located close to the Bandra Worli Sea Link, this place is frequented by couples, photographers and young folk who come to chill out near Lands Ends.

  • Location: Bandra West
  • Timings: 6am–6:30pm

Check out these pubs, clubs and other things Mumbai is famous for!

6. Sanjay Gandhi National Park – Most Visited Park in Mumbai

The Sanjay Gandhi National Park has something for people of all ages. It has got amazing walking trails, such as Shilonda, wonderful structures, such as the must-see centuries-old Kanheri Caves, as well as the opportunity for you to spot animals and birds. It is a great picnic spot for Mumbaikars.

  • Entry Fee: Rs. 15 for Indians, Rs. 200 for foreigners
  • Location: Borivali
  • Timings: 7:30 am to 5:30 pm

List of Best Places to Visit in Mumbai with Friends

1. Basilica of Our Lady of the Mount –
A Prominent Catholic Church

Mumbai is a place for friends and it has a lot of places to hang out with your buddies. The Basilica of Our Lady of the Mount is also known as the Mount Mary’s Church. It is one of the prominent Roman Catholic churches in Bandra. Situated near the sea, this church attracts thousands of devotees who come to pay homage and seek the blessings of Mother Mary.

The best time to visit the basilica is during the Bandra fair that is held in the second week of September every year.

  • Location: Bandra
  • Timings: 6:30am–8:30pm

You may also like to read about 12 iconic churches in Mumbai to visit

2. Wankhede Stadium – Famous for Hosting 2011 Cricket World Cup Final

Located in South Mumbai, the Wankhede Stadium is Mumbai’s biggest cricket ground. At this iconic stadium, you can catch cricket matches. The best time to visit is during the Indian Premier League that is held every year in April and May. Locals come to the stadium by the dozens to support their favourite team, the Mumbai Indians.

  • Location: Marine Lines

Also check Hotels near Mmrda Grounds Mumbai

3. Prithvi Theatre – Iconic Theatre Founded by Prithviraj Kapoor

Situated in the suburbs, the Prithvi Theatre is run by the Kapoor family of Bollywood. The iconic theatre has been the place where most of Bollywood’s bigwigs began their acting journey.

If you are in Mumbai and have a few hours to spare, we suggest that you visit this iconic theatre to watch a play. Afterwards, head down to the Prithvi Cafe that is located within the premises.

  • Location: Juhu

4. Carter Road – For Street Food in Mumbai

Located in Bandra West, the Carter Road is the hangout place with friends in Mumbai. Post 6 in the evening, the place is packed with bikers, cars, couples, joggers and college students who come to chill out. With the best restaurants and cafes around and good street food options, there’s no better way to spend an evening in Mumbai.

If you are a fitness buff, you can head to the Bandra Promenade for an evening walk.

  • Location: Bandra West

5. Leopold’s Café –An Iconic Café in Mumbai

This iconic café and bar in Mumbai has a strong connection to Mumbai’s nightlife and culture. With its old school décor, wood furniture and clean white walls, the café is a famous hangout spot for foreign as well as domestic tourists. The place is famous for its beer on tap and chicken chilly. You may have to wait to get a table, especially on the weekends.

  • Average Cost: Rs. 2,000 for two
  • Location: Colaba Causeway

You can also read this to check out the 17 amazing cafes in Mumbai to visit

6. Crawford Market – A Popular Shopping Centre in Mumbai

The Crawford Market is a historic shopping centre in Mumbai. At this iconic market, you can shop for a variety of products ranging from cloth to food, birds and fruits. All the shops in the market offer their products at huge discounts. You can also find imported products being sold inside the market.

Once you have finished shopping, head out of the market and make a pitstop at the iconic Badshah Falooda shop nearby.

  • Entry Fee: Free
  • Location: Mohammed Ali Road

You may also like 13 street shopping markets in Mumbai for the bargain hunters.

7. Colaba Causeway – A Shoppers Paradise

Located close to the Fort area of Mumbai, Colaba Causeway has always been a popular shopping street. For great gifts, trinkets, clothes and accessories at reasonable prices, you must head here. Adding to the fun is the quirky and innovative ways in which sellers try to market their products.

  • Entry fee: None
  • Location: Fort

List of Best Places to Visit in Mumbai at Night

1. Marine Drive – TheQueen’s Necklace

The Marine Drive starts at the north of Nariman Point and goes up to Worli. At this famous place, you will find a lot of people chilling out, especially in the evenings. Equipped with a promenade and many benches, visit this place to soak in the fresh sea breeze and to spend a few hours outdoors. The best time to visit Marine Drive is late at night when there is no traffic.

  • Entry Fee: None
  • Location: Marine Drive

You can also explore these clean and beautiful beaches in Mumbai during the weekend

2. Juhu Beach – Famous for its Chaats and Street Food

This beach is famous for its chaat and street food stalls. Go here in the evenings to enjoy long strolls and indulge in a variety of chaats. Swimming is not allowed at the beach so avoid going into the water.

There are water sports that you can enjoy at Juhu Beach. This usually takes place in the summer and Diwali vacations. It is perfect for sunset and evening stroll with friends and family. 

  • Entry Fee: None
  • Location: Juhu

3. Bandra-Worli Sea Link – An Engineering Marvel

A modern-day engineering marvel, the Bandra-Worli Sea Link is one of the coolest bridges in Mumbai. The only suspended bridge in the city, a drive through this is something that every tourist has to experience. The best time to drive through the bridge is at night when the traffic is not hectic and the street lights are on.

  • Location: Bandra, Worli

4. Gorai Beach – A Weekend Getaway from Mumbai

Gorai Beach is a great place to escape the hustle and bustle of Mumbai. This seaside village has many resorts and guest houses that are great for a short getaway. Gorai is conveniently located and you can take a BEST bus or hire an Uber/Ola cab or take a regular cab from the city to get to the beach from Mumbai.

  • Location: Gorai

5. Manori Beach – Mini Goa

Located a little away from the city centre, Manori is a cool place for a weekend getaway or a picnic. There are many resorts and holiday homes located in the area that have all the facilities for a comfortable stay.

The beach at Manori is pretty clean and you can enjoy playing sports on the hard sand. If you are looking to enjoy a few Mumbai favourites, you can find them at food stalls on the beach.

To get to the beach you have to take a ferry from the Marve Beach that is across Manori.

  • Location: Manori

Mumbai definitely is one of the most beautiful cities in India. It is a must-visit for locals and tourists alike. In case you’ve not visited Mumbai yet, now is the time to do so.

Frequently Asked Questions About Places to Visit in Mumbai

What are the places to visit in Mumbai in 2 days?

In 2 days, you can visit central Mumbai on the first day and outskirts of Mumbai on the second day. You can cover places like Gateway of India, Colaba Causeway, Marine Drive, Juhu Beach, Sanjay Gandhi National Park, Elephanta Caves and so on. 

What are the 10 top places to visit in Mumbai?

The top 10 places to visit in Mumbai are Marine Drive, Bandra-Worli Sea Link. Gateway of India, Kanheri Caves, Global Vipassana Pagoda, Siddhivinayak Temple, Nariman Point, Sanjay Gandhi National Park, Haji Ali Dargah, and Elephanta Caves. 

What are the places to visit in Mumbai in monsoon?

You can visit Marine Drive, Bandra Bandstand, Carter Road, Juhu Beach, Worli Seaface, Gateway of India, Manori Creek, and Powai Lake in Mumbai in monsoon. 

What are the places to visit in Mumbai in one day?

You can probably go shopping as Mumbai is a shopper paradise. You can start at Colaba Causeway and check out Linking Road, Fashion Street, Crawford Market, and so on. 

What are the romantic places to visit in Mumbai?

The most romantic places to visit in Mumbai are Nariman Point, Marine Drive, Bandra Bandstand, Juhu Chowpatty Beach, Juhu Chowpatty, Worli Sea Face, Carter Road, and Versova Rocky Beach.
